4. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Cookies and similar technologies help us keep Daily Math Challenge secure, reliable, and tailored to your needs. This section explains what these technologies are and how we may use them.

4.1 What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser. They allow websites to recognize your device and remember information about your visit, such as your preferences or login status. Similar technologies may include local storage, pixels, and device identifiers.

4.2 Types of cookies we may use

  • Strictly necessary cookies that are essential for the Service to function, such as maintaining your session or security features.
  • Performance and analytics cookies that help us understand how the Service is used, so we can improve stability, speed, and usability.
  • Preference cookies that remember your settings, such as time zone or interface options, to provide a more personalized experience.

4.3 Your choices

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings, including blocking or deleting them. If you choose to disable certain cookies, some features of the Service may not function as intended (for example, staying signed in or preserving your preferences).

Where required by law, we will ask for your consent before using non-essential cookies or similar technologies. You may withdraw your consent at any time using your browser settings or controls we provide.
